Chocolate is TOXIC to dogs. It contains theobromine and caffeine, which dogs metabolize very slowly; both compounds stimulate the nervous system and heart.

Severity is dose- and chocolate-type dependent: baking chocolate and dark chocolate carry the highest theobromine concentrations, followed by milk chocolate; white chocolate contains very little theobromine but still poses risk from fat and sugar. Smaller dogs reach a toxic dose from a much smaller quantity than larger dogs.

Clinical signs typically appear 6-12 hours after ingestion and include vomiting, diarrhea, restlessness, excessive thirst, increased heart rate, muscle tremors, and in severe cases seizures or collapse.

If ingestion is suspected, contact a licensed veterinarian or the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center (888-426-4435) immediately with the dog's weight, the chocolate type, and the estimated amount consumed. Do not wait for symptoms.
