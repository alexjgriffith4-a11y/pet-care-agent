Based on the information provided — a roughly 20 lb dog vomiting for approximately 2 days — this situation warrants prompt veterinary attention. Urgency: VET SOON.

Vomiting that persists beyond 24 hours raises concern for dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and a range of underlying causes including dietary indiscretion, gastrointestinal infection, toxin exposure, foreign body obstruction, pancreatitis, or systemic illness. A 20 lb dog has limited fluid reserves relative to larger dogs and can decompensate more quickly.

Warning signs that would escalate to VET NOW (emergency) include: inability to keep water down for more than a few hours, blood in the vomit, profound lethargy or collapse, a distended or painful abdomen, pale or tacky gums, or vomiting accompanied by persistent diarrhea.
